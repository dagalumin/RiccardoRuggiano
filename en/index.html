BETTY BEE

project
Evanescence

MAXXI museum
Rome 2007


The project plays on the body, the symbols and the iconography dear to the artist. Neck, womb, forearm and foot. Cast from life. The pieces are made of four different materials: plaster with bronze patina; latex and fibre with aluminium patina; gauze and polyresin; transparent resin.

PULCINELLA AND ATELLAN FARCES
Origins of interculturality and continuity

1 December 2012 – 6 January 2013
Temple of Pomona
Salerno

Cultural twinning programme between Salerno and Naples


"There are many similarities between theatre jesters of different epochs. Eighteenth century theories sought their lineage among the Atellan Farces and the Pulcinelli. As part of the exhibition dedicated to the traditional art of eighteenth-century Naples, the sculptor Riccardo Ruggiano presents a comparison between the Pulcinella mask of the Commedia dell’Arte and the Maccus mask of the Osco-Sabellian Theatre."

TRIONFANTE - TRIUMPHANT
Presentation of the restored Byzantine Christ

18 September 2011
Girolamini complex
Newman Room
Naples


"The sculptor Riccardo Ruggiano proposes an interpretative reconstruction of the missing arms of the Byzantine wooden Christ, in sympathy with the medieval style. The artwork, which dates back to the second half of the 13th century and has been held in the Girolamini monument complex since the 16th century, is the oldest among those present in the Oratory."

COLLEZIONE EX - EX COLLECTION
Solo exhibition

26 September – 10 October 2008
Ex Scuderie Duca D’Atri Palace
Naples

fitting
R. Ruggiano - L. D’Avino - F. A. Cabrera


"Objects of daily use, icons of contemporary consumer society, have been transformed with ironic contrast into precious and fine porcelain. Products with a short life-cycle are immortalised in the beauty of their aesthetic, ergonomic and production engineering. The exhibition, mindless of the speed of consumption, encourages reflection."
